### Problem Statement
You are an analyst for the Light & Power Company. You want to compare the electricity consumption of single-family homes in two different towns. You have collected the following data from a random sample of homes in each town:
Metric	Town 1	Town 2
Sample Size ($n$)	25	21
Sample Mean ($\bar{x}$)	$85	$68
Sample Std. Deviation ($s$)	$30	$18
At the $\alpha = 0.05$ level of significance, is there sufficient evidence of a difference in the variances of electricity consumption between the two towns? Please complete the analysis using the SPDC four-step framework. Please utilize the test statistic with the critical value.

### Model Solution
1. State: Hypotheses and Parameters
• Parameters: $\sigma_1^2$ and $\sigma_2^2$ are the true population variances of electricity consumption for single-family homes in Town 1 and Town 2, respectively.
• Hypotheses:
o $H_0: \sigma_1^2 = \sigma_2^2$ (There is no difference in variances).
o $H_a: \sigma_1^2 \neq \sigma_2^2$ (There is a difference in variances—two-tailed test).
• Significance Level: $\alpha = 0.05$.
2. Plan: Procedure and Conditions
• Procedure: Two-sample F-test for equal population variances.
• Conditions:
1. Random and Independent: Both samples are random and independent of each other.
2. Normal Populations: The populations of electricity consumption in both towns must be approximately normally distributed.
3. Do: Calculations
• Sample Statistics: Town 1 ($n_1=25, s_1^2=900$); Town 2 ($n_2=21, s_2^2=324$).
• Degrees of Freedom:
o Numerator $v_1 = n_1 - 1 = 25 - 1 = \mathbf{24}$ .
o Denominator $v_2 = n_2 - 1 = 21 - 1 = \mathbf{20}$ .
• Test Statistic ($F$):
$$F = \frac{s_1^2}{s_2^2} = \frac{30^2}{18^2} = \frac{900}{324} \approx \mathbf{2.778}$$
• Critical Value: Using the F-table for $\alpha/2 = 0.025$ with $v_1=24$ and $v_2=20$, the upper critical value is $2.35$.
4. Conclude: Interpretation
• Statistical Decision: Since the calculated test statistic $F \approx 2.778$ is greater than the critical value $2.35$, it falls into the rejection region . We Reject the null hypothesis ($H_0$) at $\alpha = 0.05$.
• Contextual Conclusion: There is sufficient evidence at the 0.05 level of significance to conclude that there is a significant difference in the variances of electricity consumption between Town 1 and Town 2.
