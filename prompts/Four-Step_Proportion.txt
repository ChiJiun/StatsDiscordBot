### Problem Statement

A nationwide retail chain reports that 47% of all customers prefer shopping with the company’s mobile app rather than in-store. A market research firm surveys 1025 randomly selected customers and records the sample proportion who prefer the mobile app.
Find the probability that the sample proportion is within 2 percentage points of the true value (47%).
Show all steps using the SPDC structure.

---

### Model Solution (Instructor’s Standard Answer)
(These are for your understanding only. DO NOT include them in your output.)

### State

A We are interested in the sampling distribution of the sample proportion p̂.
B Let p = true proportion of all customers who prefer using the company’s mobile app.
C True proportion: p=0.47
D Sample size: n=1025 
E We want to compute: P(0.45 ≤ p̂ ≤ 0.49)

### Plan

**Randomness**

- The 1025 customers are selected at random, so the sample is representative. 
- Mean of p̂: μₚ̂ = p = 0.47

**Independence**

- 1025 ≤ 0.10 × (the number of all customers in the population)
- It is reasonable to assume the company has far more than 10,250 customers.
- SD of p̂:σₚ̂ = √(0.47 × 0.53 / 1025) ≈ 0.016

**Normality**

- np = 1025(0.47) ≥ 10
- n(1–p) = 1025(0.53) ≥ 10
- Both conditions are satisfied. The sampling distribution of p̂ is approximately Normal.

- We will use a Normal model for the sampling distribution of p̂ after checking the required conditions.
- p̂ ~ N(0.47, 0.016)

### Do

A We want to get P(0.45 ≤ p̂ ≤ 0.49)
B Convert to Z-scores: P( (0.45 – 0.47) / 0.016 ≤ Z ≤ (0.49 – 0.47) / 0.016 )
C = P( –1.25 ≤ Z ≤ 1.25 )
D Using the standard Normal distribution:
E P(–1.25 ≤ Z ≤ 1.25) ≈ 0.79

### Conclude

A There is about a 0.79 probability that 
B a sample of 1025 customers 
C will produce a sample proportion 
D that is within 2 percentage points of the true population value 47%—that is, between 45% and 49%, 
E for the proportion of customers who prefer using the company’s mobile app.
