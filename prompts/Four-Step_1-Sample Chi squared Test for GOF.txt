### Problem Statement
As personnel director, you want to test the perception of fairness of three methods of performance evaluation.  Of 180 employees, 63 rated Method 1 as fair, 45 rated Method 2 as fair, 72 rated Method 3 as fair.   At the 0.05 level of significance, is there a difference in perceptions?

### Model Solution
1. State: Hypotheses and Parameters
Goal: We want to determine if there is a significant difference in the perception of fairness among the three performance evaluation methods at the $\alpha = 0.05$ significance level.
•	Parameters:
o	$p_1$: The true proportion of employees who perceive Method 1 as fair.
o	$p_2$: The true proportion of employees who perceive Method 2 as fair.
o	$p_3$: The true proportion of employees who perceive Method 3 as fair.
•	Hypotheses:
o	$H_0: p_1 = p_2 = p_3$ (The perceptions of fairness are equal across all methods).
o	$H_a$: At least one of the proportions is different (There is a difference in perceptions).
•	Significance Level: $\alpha = 0.05$.
________________________________________
2. Plan: Procedure and Conditions
Procedure: Chi-square Test for Goodness-of-Fit (specifically testing for equal proportions).
To perform this test validly, we must verify:
1.	Random: We assume the 180 employees represent a random sample of the population.
2.	Independence: The total sample size $n = 180$ is likely less than 10% of all employees in a large organization.
3.	Large Counts: All expected counts must be $\ge 5$.
o	Since $H_0$ assumes $p_1=p_2=p_3=1/3$, the expected count for each method is $E = 180 \times (1/3) = 60$.
o	Since $60 \ge 5$ for all categories, this condition is satisfied.
________________________________________
3. Do: Calculations
Data Summary:
•	Observed ($O$): $O_1 = 63, O_2 = 45, O_3 = 72$.
•	Expected ($E$): $E_1 = 60, E_2 = 60, E_3 = 60$.
Calculation:
1.	Test Statistic ($\chi^2$):
$$\chi^2 = \sum \frac{(O_i - E_i)^2}{E_i}$$
$$\chi^2 = \frac{(63-60)^2}{60} + \frac{(45-60)^2}{60} + \frac{(72-60)^2}{60}$$
$$\chi^2 = \frac{9}{60} + \frac{225}{60} + \frac{144}{60} = \frac{378}{60} = \mathbf{6.3}$$
2.	Degrees of Freedom ($df$):
$df = k - 1 = 3 - 1 = \mathbf{2}$.
3.	P-value / Critical Value:
Using the Chi-square table for $df=2, \alpha=0.05$:
o	Critical value $\chi^2_{0.05} = \mathbf{5.991}$.
o	P-value $\approx \mathbf{0.0429}$ (using a calculator).
________________________________________
4. Conclude: Interpretation
Statistical Decision:
Since the calculated test statistic $\chi^2 = 6.3$ is greater than the critical value $5.991$, it falls into the rejection region. We Reject the null hypothesis ($H_0$) at $\alpha = 0.05$.
Contextual Conclusion:
There is sufficient evidence at the 0.05 level of significance to conclude that there is a significant difference in the perceptions of fairness among the three performance evaluation methods.
