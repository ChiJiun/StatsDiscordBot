### Problem Statement
One-Sample T Test for Population Mean 
New Business Context Problem
A retail analytics firm is studying the average customer spending per transaction at a chain of convenience stores. The company believes that if the average transaction amount falls below $5, the store may not be profitable enough to maintain operations.
An analyst randomly selects 15 transactions from one store and records the spending amounts (in dollars):
4.59, 5.04, 3.29, 5.23, 4.13, 5.50, 4.83, 4.40
5.42, 6.38, 4.01, 4.66, 2.87, 5.73, 5.55
Summary statistics for the sample:
xbar = 4.775
s = 0.939
n = 15
At the alpha = 0.05 significance level, is there convincing evidence that the true mean transaction amount at this store is less than $5?

### Model Solution
State
1.Let mu represent the true mean transaction amount (in dollars) for all transactions at this store.
2.Null Hypotheses:   H0: mu = 5

3.Alternative Hypotheses:  Ha: mu < 5
4.Significance level:  alpha = 0.05
5.We test whether there is convincing statistical evidence that the true mean transaction amount is less than $5. 
________________________________________
Plan

Conditions:
1.Random Condition
The data come from 15 randomly selected transactions, so randomness condition is satisfied.
2.Independence Condition
The sample size (n = 15) is less than 10 percent of all transactions at the store, so independence is reasonable.
3.Approximately Normal Condition
Because the sample size is small (n = 15), we examine the sample distribution.
4.A boxplot or graph of the data shows no strong skewness or outliers.
5.Therefore, the conditions required for a one-sample t-test for a population mean are satisfied. We will perform a one-sample t-test for a population mean.
________________________________________
Do
Test statistic formula: t = (xbar - mu0) / (s / sqrt(n))
Substitute the values
1.4.775 - 5 = -0.225
2.0.939 / sqrt(15) ≈ 0.2429
3.t = -0.225 / 0.2429≈ -0.927
4.Degrees of freedom: df = n - 1 = 14
5.Using a t distribution with df = 14: p-value ≈ 0.185
________________________________________
Conclude
1.Compare p-valu with alpha. Since p-valu is greater than alpha (0.185 > 0.05),
2.we fail to reject H0.
3.At the 0.05 significance level, there is not convincing evidence for Ha that 
4.that the true mean transaction amount is less than $5
5.Therefore, based on this sample, we cannot conclude that the store's transaction value is low enough to threaten profitability.
